## Rules and Safety Guidelines
1.  **Check Conditions:** Always assess wind speed and direction, and water conditions (tide, currents, obstacles) before launching. Beginners should stick to light, consistent winds (5-15 knots) and calm, shallow waters.
2.  **Equipment Check:** Ensure your board, sail, mast, boom, and fin are in good condition and correctly assembled. Check leash attachment.
3.  **Personal Safety Gear:** Always wear a personal flotation device (PFD) or impact vest. Consider a helmet, especially in shallow or crowded areas.
4.  **Upwind/Downwind Rule:** Understand that you will naturally drift downwind. Practice sailing upwind to return to your starting point. Never sail directly downwind unless you have a plan to return.
5.  **Right of Way:** Learn basic right-of-way rules to avoid collisions. Generally, the starboard (right hand forward) tack has right of way over port (left hand forward) tack.
6.  **Falling Safely:** Expect to fall. Learn to fall away from the rig to avoid injury. Always keep hold of the boom or uphaul line.
7.  **Uphauling:** Master the uphaul technique to pull the sail out of the water. This is fundamental for beginners.
8.  **Self-Rescue:** Know how to self-rescue by lying on the board and paddling back, or by disassembling your rig and swimming it in if conditions worsen.
9.  **Buddy System:** Whenever possible, windsurf with a buddy or inform someone of your plans and expected return time.
10. **Respect the Environment:** Be mindful of marine life, other water users, and local regulations.

## On-Water Safety
3. **Essential Safety Gear:** Always wear a proper PFD and impact vest. Use a helmet in waves or crowded venues. Consider a harness with quick-release hook for extreme conditions.

4. **Advanced Navigation:** Master sailing upwind in strong conditions. Understand your equipment's limits and your personal "point of no return" for downwind runs.

6. **High-Speed Recovery:** Practice controlled falls at speed. Learn emergency releases and always maintain awareness of your rig's position relative to your body.

7. **Waterstart Safety:** Master waterstarts in various conditions. Know when to waterstart versus uphaul based on wind strength and sea state.

8. **Advanced Self-Rescue:** Be prepared for equipment failure. Practice self-rescue techniques in rough conditions and know how to signal for assistance.

## Responsibility
9. **Risk Assessment:** Continuously evaluate conditions and your fatigue level. Know when to push limits and when to dial it back.

10. **Environmental Stewardship:** Protect launch sites and respect local ecosystems. Be an ambassador for the sport through responsible behavior.
