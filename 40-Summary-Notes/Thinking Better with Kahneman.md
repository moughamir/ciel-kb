# Thinking Better with Kahneman

This note summarizes key concepts from Daniel Kahneman's "Thinking, Fast and Slow" and their application to personal development and note-making.

## Two Systems of Thinking

Daniel Kahneman's book introduces two systems of thinking:

-   **System 1:**
    -   **Characteristics:** Fast, intuitive, automatic.
    -   **Use Cases:** Quickly recognizing patterns, making snap judgments based on limited information (e.g., reacting to a snake).
-   **System 2:**
    -   **Characteristics:** Slow, deliberate, logical.
    -   **Use Cases:** Analyzing complex information, making reasoned decisions, solving math problems, important life decisions. Requires more effort and attention.

## Cognitive Biases

-   Systematic errors in thinking that can lead to flawed decisions.
-   Caused by emotions, social pressures, or brain limitations.

## System 1 & System 2 in Note-Making/Taking

-   **Note-Taking (System 1):** Useful for quickly jotting down important information or key points during a lecture or meeting.
-   **Note-Making (System 2):** Useful for synthesizing and organizing information from multiple sources, critically evaluating information, and developing new ideas/insights.

## Note-Taking vs. Note-Making

-   **Note-Taking:**
    -   **Purpose:** Capturing information concisely during lectures, meetings, or reading.
    -   **Method:** Recording key points, ideas, using abbreviations, bullet points, shorthand.
    -   **Focus:** Review and retention.
-   **Note-Making:**
    -   **Purpose:** Synthesizing and organizing information from multiple sources to create something new.
    -   **Method:** Developing own ideas, arguments, insights; connecting information creatively.
    -   **Focus:** Active process for deeper understanding and critical thinking.
