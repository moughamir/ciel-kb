## Talos Story (Greek Mythology)

Talos is a figure from Greek mythology, a giant bronze automaton created by the god Hephaestus to protect the island of Crete.

-   **Description:** Made with a single vein running from his neck to his ankle, filled with liquid metal.
-   **Role:** Guarded Crete, preventing invaders by hurling boulders at ships.
-   **Demise:** Encountered Jason and the Argonauts. Medea, Jason's sorceress companion, hypnotized Talos and removed the bolt keeping the liquid metal inside, leading to his collapse and death.
-   **Interpretations:** Seen as a protector, or a symbol of technology's dangers.
