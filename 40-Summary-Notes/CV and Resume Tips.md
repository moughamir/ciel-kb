# CV and Resume Tips

This note provides tips for creating an interesting and effective CV or resume.

## Tips for an Effective CV/Resume

1.  **Focus on Accomplishments:** Highlight achievements and accomplishments using specific examples and metrics, rather than just listing responsibilities.
2.  **Clear and Easy-to-Read Format:** Use headings, bullet points, and white space. Choose an easy-to-read font and keep the design simple and uncluttered.
3.  **Customize for Each Job:** Tailor the CV/resume to each specific job application, highlighting relevant skills and experiences.
4.  **Include a Personal Statement:** Use a short summary at the top to showcase professional goals, achievements, and unique value proposition.
5.  **Proofread and Edit:** Carefully proofread for typos and errors, and have someone else review it.

## CV vs. Resume

-   **CV (Curriculum Vitae):**
    -   **Length:** Typically longer and more detailed.
    -   **Usage:** Common in academic, research, or medical fields.
    -   **Content:** Comprehensive overview of education, work experience, publications, presentations, awards, etc.
    -   **Geographic:** Commonly used outside North America.
-   **Resume:**
    -   **Length:** Usually shorter and tailored specifically to the job.
    -   **Usage:** Commonly used in North America.
    -   **Content:** Highlights key skills, work experience, and accomplishments relevant to the position.
    -   **Focus:** Concise, focusing on relevant qualifications and achievements.
